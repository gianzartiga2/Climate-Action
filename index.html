The basics

What Is Climate Change?

Climate change is the long-term shift in Earth's temperatures and weather patterns. While natural changes can happen over time, today's rapid warming is mainly caused by human activities that release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

These gases trap heat, affecting oceans, forests, wildlife, food systems, water supplies, and communities around the world.

1.2°C

Global temperatures have already risen by about this much compared with pre-industrial levels.

Why it happens

Major Causes

Climate change is driven by activities that increase heat-trapping gases in the atmosphere.

Fossil Fuel Use

Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity, heat, and transport releases large amounts of carbon dioxide.

Deforestation

Clearing forests removes natural carbon storage and often releases stored carbon back into the air.

Transportation

Cars, trucks, ships, and planes powered by petroleum contribute heavily to global emissions.

Agriculture

Livestock, fertilizer use, and rice production can release methane and nitrous oxide.

Industrial Processes

Cement, steel, chemicals, and manufacturing systems consume energy and create process emissions.

What changes

Global Effects

Climate impacts are already visible across weather patterns, oceans, food systems, and public health.

Rising Temperatures

Heat waves become more intense, increasing health risks and energy demand.

Sea Level Rise

Melting ice and warming oceans threaten coastal cities, islands, and freshwater supplies.

Extreme Weather

Storms, droughts, floods, and wildfires can grow more frequent or damaging.

Biodiversity Loss

Species and habitats face pressure as temperatures, rainfall, and ocean conditions shift.

Food and Water Stress

Crop yields, fisheries, and water availability can become less reliable.
